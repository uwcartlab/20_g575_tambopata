She hopes to convince people in Tambopata of the advantages of oil palm by pointing to the high global demand for palm oil and the big profits made by producers in other areas. Although oil palm producers have been criticized for displacing smallholder farmers, Mariella’s company is committed to working with small holders.

She knows that local conservationists fear that some companies will clear primary and secondary natural forests to plant oil palm trees, or that companies will buy land from local farmers growing food, thus forcing them to clear forest elsewhere for new agricultural fields. Other opponents point out that oil palm plantations need to be large (~1,000 ha) to be profitable. In exchange, Mariella is confident that her company can offer a price premium for farmers’ sustainable oil palm production. Her company is also looking for land to purchase to develop its own plantations, and she believes her company’s larger size can optimize efficiency in their use of resources such as land, water, and energy.
